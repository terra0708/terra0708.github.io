What is the Crime of Threat? What are the Penalties?

Home Legal Areas Criminal Law Crime of Threat

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the crime of threat, which comes to the fore when a person is frightened that an unjust harm will be realized towards themselves or their relatives. This crime is regulated under Article 106 of the Turkish Penal Code and aims to protect the peace and safety of individuals.

What is the crime of threat?

The crime of threat is the frightening of a person that an unfair harm will be done to their life, bodily integrity, property, or peace, or toward those of their relatives. The legal benefit to be protected with this crime is the freedom of decision and movement of the person.

In which situations does the crime of threat occur?

The crime of threat particularly comes to the fore in the following concrete situations:

  • Explicit statements regarding life safety such as "I will kill you", "I will not let you live".
  • Frightening that physical violence will be applied.
  • Saying that the victim's house, car, or workplace will be harmed.
  • Threatening messages sent from digital media (WhatsApp, Instagram, etc.).

What are the elements of the crime of threat?

For an act to be legally considered a crime of threat:

  • Threat of Harm: There must be an unjust harm stated to be realized in the future.
  • Illegality: The stated harm must be unlawful.
  • Seriousness: The threat must be suitable to disturb the inner peace of the victim and create fear in them.

Who can be the parties to the crime?

Anyone can be the perpetrator of this crime. However, the victim of the crime can only be real persons. If threatening language is used against legal entities (companies, institutions, etc.), different crime types (for example, violation of freedom of work and labor) may come to the fore.

What is the penalty for the crime of threat?

According to TCK Art. 106/1, the penalty for the crime of threat varies according to the nature of the crime:

  • Simple Threat (Life/Bodily Integrity): Imprisonment from 6 months to 2 years.
  • Threat to Property: Imprisonment up to 6 months or a judicial fine upon complaint of the victim.

What are the qualified (aggravated) forms of the crime of threat?

In the following cases, the crime is considered "qualified" and the penalty is increased to imprisonment for 2 to 5 years:

  • The crime is committed with a weapon.
  • The person makes themselves unrecognizable (mask, etc.) or uses an unsigned letter.
  • Committed together (in participation) by more than one person.
  • Committed by taking advantage of the frightening power created by existing or assumed criminal organizations.

Is the crime of threat subject to a complaint? Is mediation possible?

The simple form of the crime (not directed at life) is subject to a complaint and is within the scope of mediation. However, threats against life and bodily integrity and qualified versions are not subject to a complaint; they are investigated ex officio by the prosecution office.

How does the investigation and prosecution process progress?

  1. A criminal complaint is made to law enforcement (police/gendarmerie) or the Prosecutor's Office.
  2. The Prosecution Office collects evidence (messages, witnesses, camera records).
  3. If sufficient suspicion is formed, an indictment is prepared and a lawsuit is filed in the Criminal Court.
  4. Hearings are held, witnesses are heard, and the material truth is researched.
  5. The court may decide on acquittal, penalty, or probation.

How long does a threat crime case take?

According to the evidence status and the workload of the court, threat cases generally result within 6 months to 1.5 years.
