What is Intentional Injury Offense? What are the Penalties?

Home Legal Areas Criminal Law Intentional Injury

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the intentional injury offense, which comes to the fore when bodily integrity is intentionally harmed. This offense is regulated under Articles 86 and 87 of the Turkish Penal Code.

What is the intentional injury offense?

Intentional injury offense is a type of crime that violates a person's bodily integrity, committed by intentionally causing pain to someone's body, or damaging their health or perception. It is one of the most common offenses in our legal system under TCK Art. 86.

In which situations does intentional injury occur?

Many situations involving physical contact can constitute the crime of intentional injury:

  • Assault acts (slapping, punching, kicking, etc.).
  • Bruises, cuts, or injuries resulting from physical intervention.
  • Interventions that directly damage the person's health.
  • Behaviors that temporarily or permanently affect the person's ability to perceive.

What are the elements of the intentional injury offense?

Three basic elements are required for the occurrence of the crime:

  • Act: Carrying out an intervention against the victim's body.
  • Intent: The perpetrator carries out this painful or health-damaging result knowingly and willingly.
  • Result: Pain must have been caused to the victim's body, or their health or perception must have been damaged.

Who can commit intentional injury and who can be the victim?

Anyone can be the perpetrator of this crime. The victim is only a real person. If the crime is committed against an ascendant (parent), descendant (child), spouse, or public official, this is a reason for increasing the penalty.

What is the penalty for intentional injury?

The penalty amount is determined by the degree of injury:

  • Simple Intentional Injury: Imprisonment from 1 to 3 years (TCK Art. 86/1).
  • Injury Resolvable with Simple Medical Intervention (SMI/BTM): Imprisonment from 4 months to 1 year or a judicial fine upon complaint of the victim (TCK Art. 86/2).

What are the qualified forms of intentional injury?

In the following cases, the penalty is increased by half (TCK Art. 86/3):

  • The crime is committed with a weapon.
  • It is committed against an ascendant, descendant, spouse, or sibling.
  • It is committed against a person who cannot defend themselves physically or mentally.
  • It is committed against a public official because of their duty.

In addition, according to TCK Art. 87; in consequences such as weakening of senses/organs, bone fracture, permanent scar on the face, or life-threatening situations, the penalty increases exponentially.

Complaint and mediation procedure

Intentional injury offenses that can be resolved with simple medical intervention are subject to a complaint and are within the scope of mediation. The complaint period is 6 months. However, qualified versions of the crime (with a weapon, against a spouse, etc.) are not subject to a complaint; they are handled ex officio as a public case.

How does the investigation and prosecution process progress?

  1. The incident is reported to the law enforcement or prosecution office.
  2. The victim is referred to the nearest health institution and a Forensic Report is obtained.
  3. The perpetrator's statement is taken and witnesses are heard.
  4. The Prosecution prepares an indictment and a lawsuit is filed in the Criminal Court.
  5. The court announces its verdict according to the evidence.

How long does an intentional injury case take?

According to the process of forensic reports and the status of evidence, these cases generally result within 6 months to 2 years.

FAQ

Common questions about assault and injury

In case both parties injure each other during a fight, the provisions of "unjust provocation" may be applied and the penalty may be reduced according to the roles and fault rates of the parties in the crime.

If the penalty given is 2 years or less and the legal conditions have been formed, a decision of Postponement of the Announcement of the Verdict (HAGB) or postponement of the prison sentence can be given.
