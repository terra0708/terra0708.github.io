WHAT DECISIONS CAN BE MADE AT THE END OF A CRIMINAL TRIAL?

Home Legal Areas Criminal Law Judgment Types

In this article, we aim to explain the types of verdicts that can be rendered by the court at the end of the criminal trial process in a short, understandable, and systematic way. In practice, people seek answers to questions such as "what does acquittal mean?", "what is a decision of no grounds for penalty?", "what does HAGB mean?".

Decisions rendered at the conclusion of criminal proceedings directly affect the defendant's legal status and often have serious consequences. Therefore, a correct understanding of the types of judgments is of great importance.

  1. What decisions can be rendered at the end of a criminal trial?
  2. What does an acquittal verdict mean?
  3. What is a conviction verdict?
  4. What is a decision of no grounds for penalty?
  5. What is HAGB (Postponement of the Announcement of the Verdict)?
  6. What do dismissal and rejection of the case mean?

What is an Acquittal Verdict?

An acquittal verdict is the determination by the court that the defendant did not commit the alleged crime or that the crime was not proven. The court can render an acquittal verdict in the following cases:

  • It is fixed that the crime was not committed by the defendant
  • There is no sufficient evidence that the crime was committed
  • The act does not constitute a crime

With an acquittal verdict, no penalty is applied to the defendant.

What is a Conviction Verdict?

A conviction verdict is the decision rendered when the defendant's commission of the crime is fixed by the court. In this case, the court may adjudge sanctions such as a prison sentence or a judicial fine. A conviction verdict is one of the decisions with the most serious consequences in criminal proceedings.

What is a Decision of No Grounds for Penalty?

A decision of no grounds for penalty (Ceza Verilmesine Yer Olmadığı) is a situation where the defendant is not punished due to some legal reasons, even though they committed the act. For example:

  • Self-defense
  • State of necessity
  • Lack of culpability (mental capacity)

this decision can be given in such cases.

What is Postponement of the Announcement of the Verdict (HAGB)?

HAGB is the non-announcement of the decision under certain conditions, even though a conviction verdict has been rendered against the defendant. In this case, the defendant is evaluated as if they have not received a penalty. A specific probation period is applied. If the defendant acts in accordance with the obligations within this period, the case disappears without producing any results.

What is a Decision to Dismiss the Case?

A decision to dismiss the case (Davanın Düşmesi) is rendered if a situation arises that prevents the continuation of the trial. For example:

  • Expiration of the statute of limitations
  • Death of the defendant
  • Withdrawal of the complaint (in crimes subject to complaint)

the case is dismissed in such cases.

What is a Decision to Reject the Case?

A decision to reject the case (Davanın Reddi) is rendered in cases such as a final decision previously rendered about the same event. In this case, the court decides that there is no need to retry the same issue.

What is Adjudging Security Measures?

In some cases, the court may adjudge security measures instead of or along with a penalty. For example:

  • Measures specific to the mentally ill
  • Deprivation of the use of certain rights

These measures aim to ensure public safety.

Importance of Decisions Rendered at the End of Criminal Trial

These decisions rendered at the end of criminal proceedings can have a direct impact on an individual's freedom, reputation, and future. Therefore, it is of great importance to follow the trial process carefully and to use legal rights correctly.

The information on this page is prepared for general awareness purposes. The types of judgments that can be rendered at the end of criminal proceedings may produce different results according to the characteristics of the concrete case. It is important to correctly evaluate the legal effects of the decisions to be rendered at the conclusion of the criminal trial and to obtain legal support appropriate for your concrete situation to avoid loss of rights.
