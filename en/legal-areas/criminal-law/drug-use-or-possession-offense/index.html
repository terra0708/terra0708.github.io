What is Drug Use or Possession Offense? What are the Penalties?

Home Legal Areas Criminal Law Drug Possession

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the offense of drug use or possession, which comes to the fore when u drugs or stimulants are possessed or used for personal use. This offense is regulated under Article 191 of the Turkish Penal Code.

What is the drug use or possession offense?

Drug use or possession offense is when a person purchases, accepts, possesses, or uses drugs for entirely personal consumption without any intention of sale or distribution. The legislator aims to protect the individual from drug addiction and to maintain public health with this crime.

In which situations does drug use or possession occur?

According to Supreme Court precedents and the law, the situations where the crime occurs are:

  • Seizure of drugs not exceeding personal use amount on the person, in their vehicle, or their residence.
  • Detection of drug use by medical means (blood, urine, etc.).
  • Receiving drugs from another person for use, with or without consideration.

What are the elements of the drug use or possession offense?

  • Object: The material found must be a drug or stimulant (confirmed by criminal report).
  • Purpose: The substance must be possessed only for personal use purpose.
  • Intent: The perpetrator possesses the substance knowing it is a drug.

Who can be the parties to the crime?

Anyone can commit this crime. Since public health is harmed rather than a specific individual, "the public" is considered the victim.

What is the penalty for drug use or possession?

According to TCK Art. 191/1, the person who commits this crime is punished with imprisonment from 2 to 5 years. However, for those caught under this article, rehabilitation-oriented measures are usually applied at the first stage instead of direct imprisonment.

Distinction between personal use and trafficking

The most important issue in practice is whether the seized substance will be evaluated within the scope of "use" or "drug trafficking" (TCK 188). Penalties for trafficking are much more severe. These criteria are used for this distinction:

  • Quantity: Whether the personal annual use limit is exceeded.
  • Possession method: Presence of precision scales, packaging materials, or small baggies.
  • Perpetrator's behavior: Whether the substance is in preparation for sale.

Probation and treatment process

In drug use offenses, it is essential to decide on Postponement of Filing a Public Case. With this decision:

  • At least 1 year of probation period is given to the individual.
  • A decision is taken for treatment if necessary to recover from addiction.
  • If the person complies with the obligations during this period and does not use drugs, the case is never filed and the file is closed (TCK 191/4).

How does the investigation and prosecution process progress?

  1. Statement is taken after the seizure and the substance is sent for criminal examination.
  2. Prosecution office gives a decision of "Postponement of Filing a Public Case" (if conditions are suitable).
  3. Probation and treatment process begins.
  4. If probation is violated (re-use or breaking obligations), the prosecution office prepares an indictment and a lawsuit is filed in the Criminal Court.

How long does a drug possession case take?

Probation processes generally take 1-2 years. Criminal cases filed in case of violation can be concluded within 6 months to 1 year depending on court density.

FAQ

Legal questions about drug offenses
