What is Mediation in Criminal Proceedings? How is it Done?

Home Legal Areas Criminal Law Mediation

In this article, we aim to explain the mediation institution, which is a requirement for the investigation and prosecution of some types of crimes in criminal procedure, in a short, understandable, and practice-oriented way. It should be noted that what is mandatory is not the mediation itself, but the completion of the mediation process positively or negatively.

Mediation is an important institution that ensures the resolution of disputes especially between parties without going to the investigation and prosecution process, and when managed correctly, it can provide serious advantages in terms of both time and cost.

1. What is Mediation?

Mediation is a criminal procedure institution that ensures the resolution of the dispute outside the court and prosecution by bringing the suspect and the victim together through a mediator in certain crimes. The aim of this institution is to resolve the dispute between the parties, thereby both eliminating the victim's harm and reducing the judicial workload.

2. Which Crimes are Subject to Mediation?

Mediation is applied only for crimes clearly specified in the law. Generally:

  • Simple intentional injury
  • Threat (simple form)
  • Damage to property
  • Violation of the immunity of the residence
  • Fraud (simple form)

Serious crimes and crimes that seriously concern public order (e.g., drug trafficking, crimes against the constitutional order, etc.) are not within the scope of mediation.

3. How Does the Mediation Process Work?

When it is understood that the crime is within the scope of mediation, the file is sent to the Mediation Bureau. The process proceeds with the following steps:

  • Appointment of a Mediator: A legal mediator deals with the file.
  • Offer Stage: The mediator reaches out to the parties and asks if they want to mediate. The offer must be answered within 3 days; if not answered, it is considered rejected.
  • Meetings: If both parties accept, meetings start. These meetings are completely confidential.
  • Report: At the end of the process, a positive or negative report is issued and submitted to the prosecution/court.

4. What Happens if Mediation is Successful?

  • A public case is not filed against the suspect/defendant or the case already opened is dropped.
  • If there is an "act" (payment, apology, donation, etc.) decided as a result of mediation, the file is completely closed when it is fulfilled.
  • Most importantly, the closing of the file as a result of mediation does not appear in the criminal record.

5. What Happens if Mediation Fails?

If the parties do not agree, the trial process continues from where it left off. Nothing said in the meetings at the mediation bureau can be used as evidence against the parties in the continuation of the case.

FAQ

Curiosities About Mediation

Do I have to accept the mediation offer?

No. Mediation is based entirely on a voluntary basis. Parties can reject mediation and demand the trial to continue.

Can a lawsuit be filed again after mediation is achieved?

No. When mediation is achieved and the requirements are met, a criminal case cannot be filed again due to the same incident.

The information on this page is for general awareness purposes. The mediation process may produce different legal results according to the characteristics of the concrete case. It is recommended to get support from an expert criminal lawyer.
