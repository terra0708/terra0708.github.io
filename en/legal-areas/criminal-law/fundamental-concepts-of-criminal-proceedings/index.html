What are the Fundamental Concepts of Criminal Proceedings?

Home Legal Areas Criminal Law Key Concepts

In this article, we aim to explain the basic concepts frequently encountered and often confused in the criminal procedure in a short, understandable, and systematic way. In practice, people look for answers to questions such as "what is the difference between a suspect and a defendant?", "what does defense counsel mean?", "what is an indictment?".

A correct understanding of the concepts used in criminal proceedings is of great importance for the healthy follow-up of the process. In this context, the most common basic concepts are discussed.

  1. Suspect & Defendant
  2. Victim & Intervenor
  3. Counsel & Attorney
  4. Prosecutor & Judge
  5. Stages & Trial
  6. Indictment & Evidence
  7. Custody & Arrest
  8. Verdicts & Appeals

1. Difference Between Suspect and Defendant?

  • Suspect: A person under suspicion of committing a crime and is in the investigation stage. The prosecution carries out the research at this stage.
  • Defendant: A person against whom a criminal case has been filed upon the acceptance of the indictment. This title is used in the prosecution (court) stage.

2. What is Victim and Intervenor (Complainant)?

  • Victim: The person who directly suffers harm from the crime committed.
  • Intervenor: The title the victim gains by actively participating in the criminal case by applying to the court. They become a party to the case.

3. Difference Between Defense Counsel and Attorney?

  • Defense Counsel (Müdafi): The lawyer of the suspect or defendant in the criminal proceeding. Represents the defense.
  • Attorney (Vekil): The lawyer of the victim or intervenor. Protects the victim's rights and contributes to the prosecution.

4. Public Prosecutor and Judge

  • Public Prosecutor: Acts on behalf of the public, investigates crimes, collects evidence, and issues indictments if necessary.
  • Judge: Manages the trial independently and impartially, evaluates evidence, and renders the final decision (judgment).

5. Investigation and Prosecution Stages?

  • Investigation: The first stage carried out by the prosecution, from the suspicion of crime to the acceptance of the indictment.
  • Prosecution (Trial): The trial process held before the court, starting with the acceptance of the indictment.

6. Indictment and Evidence

  • Indictment: The official document prepared by the prosecutor when they reach a conviction of crime, determining the boundaries of the case.
  • Evidence: Any legally obtained finding (witness testimony, camera records, expert reports, etc.) that proves the reality of an event.

7. Difference Between Custody and Arrest?

  • Custody: Temporary holding at the police station to collect evidence within the scope of investigation (usually 24-96 hours).
  • Arrest: Holding in prison by court decision if there is strong suspicion of crime and possibility of fleeing/destroying evidence. It is an exceptional measure.

8. Court Verdicts: Acquittal, Conviction, and HAGB

  • Acquittal: Determination of the defendant's innocence and their exoneration.
  • Conviction: Finding the crime fixed and giving a penalty.
  • HAGB (Postponement of Announcement of Verdict): Not announcing the verdict under certain conditions; if conditions are met, it drops without appearing in the record.

9. What are Appeal and Cassation?

  • Appeal (İstinaf): Examination of the decision by the Regional Court of Justice in terms of both facts and law.
  • Cassation (Temyiz): Supervision of the decision by the Supreme Court only in terms of "compliance with the law".
Important Note

A Correct Understanding Prevents Loss of Rights

In criminal proceedings, every concept has legal consequences that can affect an individual's freedom. Differentiating between suspect-defendant, counsel-attorney, and investigation-prosecution is vital for a correct interpretation of the process.

The information on this page is for general awareness. Concepts used in criminal proceedings may produce different legal results according to the characteristics of the concrete case. Professional legal support is essential.
