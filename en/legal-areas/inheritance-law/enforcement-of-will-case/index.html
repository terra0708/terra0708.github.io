Action for Enforcement of a Will: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Inheritance Law Enforcement of a Will

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the action for enforcement of a will, which comes to the agenda for the purpose of detecting the necessity of legal implementation and fulfillment of the content of the will organized by the testator. In practice, this case is also referred to as fulfillment of the will or implementation of the will. this process ensures the protection of the rights of the will creditors and the determination of the necessity of the fulfillment of the will within the scope of Turkish Civil Code articles 600 and following. In this context, issues most wondered in practice such as what an action for enforcement of a will is, in what cases it can be filed, by whom it can be filed, which court is competent, and how the litigation process progresses are addressed.

1. What is an action for enforcement of a will?

An action for enforcement of a will is a declaratory judgment case filed with the request to determine that the contents of the will organized by the testator must be implemented and fulfilled. This point is often confused in practice.

To quote the Supreme Court's simple expression: "Enforcement of a will consists of detecting that the will has been opened and not objected to, or that the objections made have remained inconclusive. The will creditor must ask for the transfer of possession for the transfer of movable property if the right subject to the will is movable. If the possession is not given despite the decision, ownership is acquired through forced execution." (Supreme Court, 3rd HD, 07/07/2014 T., 2014/3983 E., 2014/11049 K.)

In other words, the will creditor ensures the determination of his/her entitlement by filing this lawsuit. Then, he/she requests the fulfillment of the right from the party liable for the will.

This lawsuit provides:

  • Legal basis for fulfilling rights and obligations in the will
  • Protection of will creditors' rights
  • Actual implementation of the testator's last wishes

This matter is based on Turkish Civil Code Art. 600 and subsequent provisions.

2. In which cases is it filed?

An enforcement action is filed especially when:

  • The will has been opened and finalized but not fulfilled
  • The will creditor's right is not delivered
  • Heirs fail to implement the will
  • Transfer of property subject to the will is not carried out

3. Who can file this lawsuit?

This case can usually be filed by:

  • Will creditors
  • Appointed heirs
  • Persons in whose favor a disposition has been made

4. Against whom is it filed?

It is generally filed against:

  • Statutory heirs
  • Persons responsible for fulfilling the will

5. Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Civil Court of First Instance (Asliye Hukuk Mahkemesi).

The authorized court is the court of the last residence of the testator (TCC art. 576).

6. Is there a time limit?

The right of the will creditor to sue expires ten years after he/she learns of the disposition or from the date when the obligation becomes due.

7. What to do before filing?

The following are prerequisites:

  • The will must have been opened
  • The will must be final (no pending annulment cases)
  • Notifications must have been made to relevant parties

8. How does the process progress?

  1. Lawsuit is filed at the Civil Court of First Instance.
  2. Petition is served to the parties.
  3. Evidence is collected.
  4. Expert examination is conducted if necessary.
  5. The court decides on the fulfillment of the will.

9. How long does it take?

The duration varies, but generally results in 6 months up to 2 years. Delays are usually due to the high number of parties or complexity of assets.
