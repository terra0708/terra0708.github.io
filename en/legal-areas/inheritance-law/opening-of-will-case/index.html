What Is the Proceeding to Open a Will and How Does It Work?

Home Legal Areas Inheritance Law Opening of a Will

This article explains, in brief and plain terms, the opening of a will under Turkish law: the process by which a will drawn up by the deceased is brought to light after death, declared valid where applicable, and notified to interested persons before the peace civil court. The framework is set out in Turkish Civil Code Article 596 and following provisions, which enable the peace judge to open the will, have it read to those concerned, and give rise to legal consequences. Below we address what the proceeding involves, when it arises, who may request it, which court has jurisdiction, and how the process typically unfolds.

What is the proceeding to open a will?

Opening a will is the procedure in which a will drawn up by the deceased is submitted after death to the peace civil court, opened, read to interested persons, and notified to them.

Through this procedure:

  • The content of the will is officially established
  • Heirs and interested parties are informed
  • The will can produce enforceable effects

This rests on Turkish Civil Code Art. 596 and following provisions.

When does opening of a will arise?

Opening of a will typically arises when:

  • The deceased has passed away
  • A will exists
  • The will is submitted to the competent authorities
  • The content must be communicated to interested persons

By law, anyone holding the will must deliver it to the peace judge without delay (TCC Art. 595).

Who may request opening of the will?

Opening may be requested by:

  • Statutory heirs
  • Appointed heirs
  • Legatees
  • Other interested persons

Where the will reaches the court, the peace judge may also carry out the procedure ex officio.

Is it directed against a defendant?

This is not a typical adversarial lawsuit against a named defendant.

It is a non-contentious judicial matter (Civil Procedure Code Art. 382 et seq.) conducted with the participation of interested persons.

Which court is competent and has jurisdiction?

The competent court is the Peace Civil Court.

Jurisdiction generally lies with the court of the deceased’s last place of residence (TCC Art. 596; CPC Art. 11).

Is there a specific limitation period to request opening?

There is no special peremptory “filing period” solely for this request.

However:

  • Anyone holding the will must deliver it to the court without delay (TCC Art. 595).
  • After receiving the will, the court carries out the opening as soon as practicable.

What should be prepared before requesting opening?

The following usually helps the process move smoothly:

  • Locating and obtaining the will
  • Identifying where the will was kept
  • Obtaining the death certificate
  • Preparing heirship information where available

How does the process work?

Typical steps include:

  1. The will is delivered to the peace civil court.
  2. The court summons interested persons to a hearing.
  3. The will is opened and read before the judge.
  4. A record is drawn up.
  5. Notifications are served on interested persons.

Once completed, the content of the will becomes legally clear.

How long does it take?

In practice, opening of a will often concludes within roughly one to three months.

Delays are often due to:

  • Difficulty identifying interested persons
  • Lengthy notification procedures
  • Late submission of the will
