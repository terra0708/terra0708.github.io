Action for Annulment of a Will: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Inheritance Law Annulment of a Will

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the action for annulment of a will, which comes to the agenda in cases where the will organized by the testator is unlawful, does not bear the validity conditions or is impaired by certain reasons. This lawsuit is regulated within the scope of articles 557 and following of the Turkish Civil Code and ensures the elimination of legal results by detecting the invalidity of the will. In this context, issues most wondered in practice such as what an action for annulment of a will is, in what cases it can be filed, by whom it can be filed, which court is competent, and how the litigation process progresses are addressed.

1. What is an action for annulment of a will?

An action for annulment of a will is a type of lawsuit filed for the purpose of detecting the invalidity of a will organized by the testator because it does not bear the validity conditions stipulated in the law or is unlawful.

With this lawsuit:

  • The legal validity of the will is eliminated
  • Rights and claims based on the will become invalid
  • The legal ground for inheritance distribution to be made according to statutory inheritance provisions is provided

This matter is based on Turkish Civil Code Art. 557 and following provisions.

2. In which cases is it filed?

An annulment action is filed in the following cases:

  • The testator is incapacitated (lack of power of discernment)
  • The will was made as a result of error, fraud, duress or threat
  • The content of the will is contrary to law or ethics
  • The will was made without complying with formal requirements

3. Who can file this lawsuit?

  • Statutory heirs
  • Appointed heirs
  • Will creditors
  • Persons who have an interest in the annulment of the will

4. Against whom is it filed?

  • Persons benefiting from the will
  • Appointed heirs
  • Persons to whom a disposition has been made

5. Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Civil Court of First Instance (Asliye Hukuk Mahkemesi).

The authorized court is the court of the last residence of the testator (TCC art. 576).

6. Is there a time limit?

Specific periods are stipulated in the law for the annulment of a will:

  • Lawsuit must be filed within 1 year after learning of the reason and the will.
  • In any case, the right to sue expires 10 years after opening the will.
  • If the defendants are not in good faith, this period is extended to 20 years.

These are statutes of repose (hak düşürücü süre) according to the Turkish Civil Code.

7. What to do before filing?

  • The will must have been opened and learned
  • The reason for annulment must be concretely determined
  • Evidence (witnesses, documents, etc.) must be collected

8. How does the process progress?

  1. Lawsuit is filed at the Civil Court of First Instance.
  2. Petition is served to the parties.
  3. Evidence is collected and witnesses are heard.
  4. Expert examination is conducted if necessary.
  5. The court decides whether the will is annulled or valid.

9. How long does it take?

Generally, it results in 1 to 3 years. Delays occur due to the number of parties, complexity of evidence, and expert witness processes.
