Rejection of Inheritance: What is it and How is it Done?

Home Legal Areas Inheritance Law Rejection of Inheritance

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the rejection of inheritance (reddi miras), which comes to the agenda in case the inheritance is insolvent or the heirs do not want to accept the inheritance after the death of the testator. In practice, this process is generally divided into two as constructive rejection and actual rejection. In this article, the institution of actual rejection of inheritance will be examined. This process is regulated within the scope of articles 605 and following of the Turkish Civil Code and ensures that heirs are not responsible for the debts of the estate by rejecting the inheritance.

What is rejection of inheritance?

Rejection of inheritance is the process by which an heir declares that he/she does not accept the inheritance passing to him/her after the death of the testator. With this process:

  • The heir is not responsible for the debts related to the estate.
  • The status of heir is eliminated retroactively.
  • The inheritance passes to other heirs or descendants.

This matter is based on Turkish Civil Code Art. 605 and subsequent provisions.

In which cases is it done?

  • The estate is insolvent (deeply in debt)
  • The testator's debts are too high
  • The heir does not want to be responsible for debts
  • The inheritance has no economic value

Who can reject inheritance?

It can be done by:

  • Statutory heirs
  • Appointed heirs

Each heir can reject the inheritance independently in terms of his/her own share.

To whom is it made?

Rejection of inheritance is not a classic contested lawsuit against a defendant. It is a declaration procedure in the nature of non-contentious jurisdiction and is made to the civil court of peace.

Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Civil Court of Peace (Sulh Hukuk Mahkemesi). The authorized court is the court of the last residence of the testator (TCC art. 609).

Is there a time limit?

The period determined by law for the rejection of inheritance is 3 months. This period starts from:

  • For statutory heirs, from the date they learn of the death.
  • For appointed heirs, from the date the will is notified to them.

This is a statute of repose. It is important to note that if the testator is clearly insolvent at the date of death, the inheritance is deemed rejected automatically (TCC art. 605). This is called constructive rejection (hükmen red).

What to do before rejecting?

  • Investigating the debt status of the inheritance
  • Examining bank and execution records
  • Determining the assets related to the estate

How does the process progress?

  1. Application is made to the Civil Court of Peace.
  2. Declaration of rejection is made verbally or in writing.
  3. The declaration is recorded in the minutes.
  4. The rejection process is registered.

How long does it take?

Rejection of inheritance is usually completed within the same day or a few days. However, the duration may change depending on court density.
