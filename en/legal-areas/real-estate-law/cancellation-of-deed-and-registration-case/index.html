Cancellation of Title Deed and Registration Case due to Muris Muvazaası: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Immovable Property Law Title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the title deed cancellation and registration case due to collusive transfer by the testator (muris muvazaası), filed when heirs suffer loss of rights due to transactions made by the testator during their lifetime which were shown as sales despite being donations in reality. In practice, this case is also known as "case for smuggling assets from inheritance" or "muris muvazaası case". This case ensures the invalidity of transactions that are donations in reality and the correction of the title deed within the framework of the collusion provisions of the Turkish Code of Obligations and the precedents of the Court of Cassation. In this context, the most wondered issues in practice such as what a muris muvazaası case is, in what cases it can be filed, by whom it can be filed, which court is competent, and how the litigation process progresses are addressed.

What is a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası?

This is a performance action filed to cancel transactions made by the testator to smuggle assets from heirs, which were donations in reality but shown as sales, and to register the real estate in the name of the real right owners. With this lawsuit:

  • The collusive transaction is invalid.
  • The title deed record is cancelled.
  • The real estate is registered in the name of the heirs.

This case is based on the principle of collusion (danışıklı işlem) and established precedents of the Court of Cassation.

In which situations is a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası filed?

This case is especially filed in the following situations:

  • The testator transferring their real estate to an heir or a third person
  • The transfer transaction being shown as a sale despite being a donation in reality
  • Depriving other heirs of their inheritance rights
  • The existence of intent to deceive heirs in the transaction

This lawsuit aims to ensure equity among heirs.

Who can file a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası?

This lawsuit can be filed by all heirs whose inheritance rights have been violated.

Against whom is a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası filed?

This lawsuit is filed against:

  • The testator transferring their real estate to an heir or a third person
  • The transfer transaction being shown as a sale despite being a donation in reality
  • Depriving other heirs of their inheritance rights
  • The existence of intent to deceive heirs in the transaction

It is a performance action within the scope of contested litigation and is a constitutive action as it creates a new legal situation.

What is the competent and authorized court in this case?

The competent court is the Civil Court of General Jurisdiction (Asliye Hukuk Mahkemesi). The authorized court is the court where the real estate is located.

Is there a time limit for a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası?

In title deed cancellation and registration cases based on muris muvazaası, statute of limitations generally does not apply. Therefore, heirs can always assert their rights.

What should be done before filing a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası?

The following issues are important before filing a lawsuit:

  • Examination of title deed records
  • Investigation of the true nature of the sale transaction
  • Reviewing bank records and payment documents
  • Determination of witness evidence

In this type of lawsuit, proof is of great importance.

How does the process progress in a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası?

The lawsuit process generally consists of the following stages:

  1. A lawsuit is filed in the Civil Court of General Jurisdiction.
  2. Statements of the parties are taken.
  3. Title deed records are examined.
  4. Witnesses are heard.
  5. Expert examination is conducted if necessary.
  6. The court evaluates whether there is collusion.
  7. Title deed cancellation and registration may be decided.

How long does a title deed cancellation and registration case due to muris muvazaası take?

The duration of the case varies based on the specifics of the concrete case, but results are generally achieved within **1 to 3 years** in practice. Condition of evidence and trial process can extend the duration.
