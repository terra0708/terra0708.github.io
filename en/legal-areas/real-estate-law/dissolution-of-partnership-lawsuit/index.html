Dissolution of Partnership (Partition) Case: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Immovable Property Law Dissolution of Partnership

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the dissolution of partnership (partition action) case filed to terminate the partnership on a real estate or property. This case is also known in practice as the "partition action by sale". This case ensures the termination of the partnership in properties subject to shared or joint ownership within the framework of the provisions of the Turkish Civil Code.

What is a dissolution of partnership (partition) case?

A dissolution of partnership case is a lawsuit filed to terminate shared or joint ownership on a property and to dissolve the partnership through physical division or sale. With this case:

  • The partnership is legally terminated.
  • The property is physically shared or sold.
  • The joint ownership relationship between the parties ends.

This case is based on the shared ownership provisions of the Turkish Civil Code.

In which cases is it filed?

This case is particularly filed in the following situations:

  • Formation of joint ownership through inheritance.
  • Disagreement between partners regarding the management or sale of the property.
  • Joint use of the real estate not being factually possible for all partners.
  • Continuation of the partnership becoming difficult for the parties.

Who can file this lawsuit?

This case can be filed by any of the partners alone. It is not mandatory for all partners to act together to file a lawsuit.

Against whom is it filed?

The case must be filed against all other persons who appear as shareholders/partners in the title deed. The absence of any partner in the case is an obstacle to the hearing of the case. It is a constitutive action.

Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Civil Court of Peace (Sulh Hukuk Mahkemesi). The authorized court is the court where the real estate (property) is located.

Is there a time limit for this case?

There is no specific statute of limitations or prescriptive period determined in the law for dissolution of partnership cases. It can be filed at any time as long as the partnership continues.

What to do before filing?

  • Examination of current title deed records and certificates of inheritance.
  • Clear determination of the list of partners and share ratios.
  • Analysis of "partition in kind" possibility considering the zoning status.
  • Evaluation of the possibility of settlement (agreement) to avoid unnecessary litigation expenses.

How does the process progress?

  1. A lawsuit petition is submitted to the Civil Court of Peace.
  2. The petition is served to all partners and they are included in the case.
  3. A discovery is made on-site by the court and an expert report is requested.
  4. It is first examined whether "partition in kind" (physical division) is possible.
  5. If division is not possible, a decision is made to sell the property through "public auction".
  6. When the sale takes place, costs are deducted and the amount is distributed in proportion to the shares.

How long does it take?

Due to the high number of partners, notification processes, and sale phases, these cases generally result within 1 to 2 years.
