Work Accident Determination Case: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Labor Law Work Accident Determination

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the work accident determination case, which is filed to determine whether an event an employee has suffered is legally considered a work accident. This lawsuit ensures that the event is determined to be a work accident by court decision within the framework of the definition and scope of work accident under Article 13 of the Social Insurance and General Health Insurance Law No. 5510. In this context, issues most wondered in practice such as what a work accident determination case is, in what cases it can be filed, by whom it can be filed, which court is competent, and how the litigation process progresses are addressed.

What is a work accident determination case?

A work accident determination case is a declaratory action filed for the court to determine whether an event an employee has suffered is in the nature of a work accident. With this lawsuit:

  • Whether the event is a work accident is legally determined.
  • The employee's social security rights (pension, etc.) are secured.
  • A legal basis is created for future material and moral compensation lawsuits.

This matter is based on Law No. 5510 Art. 13.

In which cases is it filed?

This lawsuit is particularly applied in the following disputes:

  • SSI (Social Security Institution) not accepting the work accident
  • Dispute with the institution on whether the event counts as a work accident
  • Official records not being kept or being incomplete despite the accident

Who can file this lawsuit?

Generally, the following have the capacity to sue:

  • The employee who had the work accident
  • Legal beneficiaries (heirs) in case of the employee's death

Against whom is it filed?

This lawsuit must be filed against:

  • The employer at the time of the accident
  • The Social Security Institution (SSI/SGK)

Both must be defendants for the decision to be recorded in the institution's official files.

Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Labor Court. Usually, the authorized court is where the employer resides or where the accident occurred.

Is there a time limit?

As it concerns social security rights, it is generally not subject to strict statutes of repose. However, a 10-year general statute of limitations from the date of the accident is usually applied in practice.

What to do before filing?

Preparation is vital for the burden of proof:

  • Immediate notification of the event to SSI and relevant authorities (police, gendarmerie)
  • Awaiting the result of the investigation by SSI inspectors
  • Obtaining reports and documents related to the moment of the event
  • Collecting hospital entry records and health reports
  • Identifying witnesses who saw the event

How does the process progress?

  1. A lawsuit petition is submitted to the Labor Court.
  2. The petition is served to the defendants (Employer and SSI).
  3. The SSI accident investigation file is summoned.
  4. Witnesses are heard during the hearings.
  5. Expert examination is conducted on medical and technical issues.
  6. The court rules whether the event is a work accident.

How long does it take?

Depending on the speed of evidence collection and expert processes, it generally takes 6 months to 1.5 years. High court appeals can extend this.
