Work Accident Determination Case: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Labor and Social Security Law Work Accident Determination Case

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the work accident determination case, which is filed to determine whether an event an employee has suffered is legally considered a work accident. This lawsuit ensures that the event is determined to be a work accident by court decision within the framework of the definition and scope of work accident under Article 13 of the Social Insurance and General Health Insurance Law No. 5510. In this context, issues most wondered in practice such as what a work accident determination case is, in what cases it can be filed, by whom it can be filed, which court is competent, and how the litigation process progresses are addressed.

What is a work accident determination case?

A work accident determination case is a declaratory action filed for the court to determine whether an event an employee has suffered is in the nature of a work accident. With this lawsuit:

  • Whether the event is a work accident is legally determined.
  • The employee's social security rights are secured.
  • A legal basis is created for future compensation lawsuits.

This matter is based on Law No. 5510 Art. 13.

In which cases is a work accident determination case filed?

A work accident determination case is particularly filed in the following cases:

  • SSI not accepting the work accident
  • Dispute on whether the event counts as a work accident
  • Official records not being kept despite the accident

This lawsuit ensures legal recognition of the work accident.

Who can file a work accident determination lawsuit?

This lawsuit can generally be filed by:

  • The employee who had the work accident
  • Legal beneficiaries in case of death

Against whom is a work accident determination case filed?

A work accident determination case is filed against:

  • The employer
  • The Social Security Institution (SSI/SGK)

This lawsuit is a declaratory action within the scope of contentious jurisdiction.

What is the competent and authorized court for a work accident determination case?

The competent court is the Labor Court. Usually, the authorized court is where the employer resides or where the work was performed.

Is there a time limit for a work accident determination case?

Since it concerns social security rights, a work accident determination case is generally subject to the statute of limitations. In practice, a 10-year statute of limitations from the date of the accident is accepted. However, different evaluations can be made depending on the specifics of the case.

What should be done before filing a work accident determination lawsuit?

Before filing a lawsuit, the following are important:

  • Notification of the event to SSI
  • Obtaining the results of the investigation to be conducted by SSI
  • Obtaining reports and documents related to the event
  • Collecting hospital and health records
  • Identifying witnesses

These preparations are of great importance for the proof of the case.

How does the work accident determination process progress?

The work accident determination process generally consists of the following stages:

  • A lawsuit is filed in the Labor Court.
  • The lawsuit petition is served to the parties.
  • SSI records are examined.
  • Witnesses are heard.
  • Expert examination is conducted if necessary.
  • The court determines whether the event was a work accident.

How long does a work accident determination case take?

While the duration varies based on the specifics of the case, it usually concludes within 6 months to 1.5 years in practice. The duration may extend depending on the status of evidence and expert processes.
