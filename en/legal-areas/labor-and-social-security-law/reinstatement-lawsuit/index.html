Reinstatement Lawsuit: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Labor Law Reinstatement Lawsuit

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the reinstatement lawsuit, which employees whose employment contracts have been terminated without a valid reason can apply to return to their jobs. This lawsuit is regulated under articles 18 and following of the Labor Law No. 4857 and provides protection for the employee within the framework of job security provisions. In this context, issues most wondered in practice such as what a reinstatement lawsuit is, in what cases it can be filed, by whom it can be filed, which court is competent, and how the litigation process progresses are addressed.

What is a reinstatement lawsuit?

A reinstatement lawsuit is a case filed by an employee whose employment contract has been terminated by the employer without a valid reason, with the aim of returning to work. With this lawsuit:

  • The invalidity of the termination is determined.
  • Reinstatement of the employee is decided.
  • Compensation for the idle period and non-reinstatement compensation are awarded.

This matter is based on Labor Law No. 4857 Art. 18 and following provisions.

In which cases is it filed?

  • Termination of the worker without a valid reason
  • Performance or behavioral grounds not reflecting reality
  • Arbitrary termination by the employer
  • Failure to comply with procedure during termination

However, for this lawsuit to be filed, the employee must be within the scope of job security.

Who can file this lawsuit?

To file a reinstatement lawsuit, the following conditions must be met:

  • At least 30 employees must be working at the workplace.
  • The employee must have at least 6 months of seniority.
  • The employee must be working with an indefinite-term employment contract.

Employees meeting these criteria can file a reinstatement lawsuit if the termination is unfair.

Against whom is it filed?

A reinstatement lawsuit is filed against the employer. It is a contested litigation matter.

Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Labor Court (İş Mahkemesi). The authorized court is the court of the employer's residence or the place where the work was performed.

Is there a time limit?

Strict deadlines are set for reinstatement lawsuits:

  • Application for mediation must be made within 1 month from the notification of termination.
  • If mediation results in disagreement, the lawsuit must be filed within 2 weeks from the date of the final mediation report.

These are statutes of repose (hak düşürücü süre).

What to do before filing?

  • Mandatory application to a mediator
  • Legal examination of the termination notice
  • Preparation of employment contract, payrolls, and other documents

The lawsuit cannot be filed without completing the mandatory mediation process.

How does the process progress?

  1. Application to a mediator.
  2. If no agreement, filing a lawsuit in the Labor Court.
  3. Submission of statements and evidence.
  4. Hearing witnesses and expert examination.
  5. Evaluation of whether the termination is valid.
  6. Judgment on reinstatement if termination is found invalid.

How long does it take?

The duration varies, but results are generally achieved within 6 months to 1 year. Appeal processes can extend this timeframe.

FAQ

Common questions about reinstatement

Reinstatement is ordered, along with up to 4 months of idle period wages and non-reinstatement compensation.

The employer must pay non-reinstatement compensation, amounting to 4 to 8 months' wages as determined by the court.

Working at a workplace with 30+ employees, having 6 months of seniority, and an indefinite contract. If the employer has multiple workplaces in the same industry, the total number of employees is considered.

The information on this page is for general awareness purposes. Since rights and obligations between employees and employers vary case by case, managing the process with an expert labor lawyer prevents loss of rights.
