Contested Divorce Case: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Family Law Contested Divorce

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the divorce case filed due to the irretrievable breakdown of the marriage, which makes the marriage unbearable for the parties. In practice, this case is also known as "divorce case due to severe incompatibility".

What is a divorce case due to the irretrievable breakdown of marriage?

This is a divorce case filed in the event that the marriage union between the spouses is broken to such an extent that they cannot be expected to continue their common life. With this legal process:

  • Legal termination of the marriage union is ensured.
  • Alimony (temporary, poverty, participation) and pecuniary-non-pecuniary compensation requests are decided.
  • Custody of joint children and establishment of personal relationships come to the fore.
  • Basic principles regarding the property division regime are determined.

This matter is based on the provision of Turkish Civil Code Art. 166/1.

In which situations is a contested divorce filed?

Some basic situations indicating that the marriage union has been shaken to its foundation are:

  • Persistent arguments, disrespect, and incompatibility between spouses.
  • Behavior shaking the trust, insults, or physical violence.
  • Complete breakdown of emotion and ideational unity, effectively ending the marriage.
  • One of the spouses failing to fulfill marriage responsibilities.

Who can file for divorce?

This case can be filed by each of the spouses who asserts that common life has become unbearable. It is legally more advantageous for the party who is faultless or whose fault is less to file the case.

Against whom is a contested divorce filed?

The case is directed against the other spouse. It is a constitutive action in terms of terminating the marriage union, and an action for performance in terms of alimony and compensation requests.

Which is the competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Family Court. The authorized court is the court of the place of residence of one of the spouses or where the spouses last lived together for six months before the case.

Is there a time limit to file for divorce?

In cases based on the general cause of irretrievable breakdown of marriage, there is no special prescriptive period determined by law. However, if events took place a long time ago, it can be considered as "forgiven", so filing the case within a reasonable time is important for proof.

What should be done before filing for divorce?

To avoid loss of rights, the following should be planned:

  • Chronological determination of concrete events with their dates.
  • Identification of persons who witnessed the events (relatives, neighbors, friends).
  • Preparation of evidence such as medical reports, message records, bank statements.
  • Clarification of priorities regarding alimony amount, compensation claims, and custody.

How does the divorce process progress?

  1. A comprehensive lawsuit petition is submitted to the Family Court.
  2. The process of exchange of petitions is completed.
  3. A preliminary examination hearing is held by the court and points of dispute are determined.
  4. In the investigation phase, evidence is collected and witnesses are heard.
  5. The court decides on divorce, compensation, alimony, and custody by determining fault ratios.

How long does a contested divorce take?

Depending on the workload of the courts, the number of witnesses, and the time to collect evidence, contested cases generally result within 1.5 to 3 years.
