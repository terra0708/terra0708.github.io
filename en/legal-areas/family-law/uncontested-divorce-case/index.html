Uncontested Divorce Case: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Family Law Uncontested Divorce

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the uncontested divorce case filed when the spouses reach an agreement on divorce and the consequences of divorce. In practice, this case is also known as "single-session divorce".

What is an uncontested divorce case?

An uncontested divorce case; it is a divorce case filed in the event that the spouses reach a full agreement on the divorce and the financial consequences of the divorce and the situation of the children. With this type of case:

  • Legal termination of the marriage union is ensured in a very short time (usually in a single session).
  • Disputes between the parties are resolved with a common "protocol".
  • The litigation process is optimized in terms of both cost and time.

This matter is based on the provision of Turkish Civil Code Art. 166/3.

In which situations is an uncontested divorce filed?

According to the law, for an uncontested divorce case to be filed, these three conditions must coexist:

  • 1 Year Condition: The marriage must have lasted at least 1 year.
  • Common Will: Spouses must be in full agreement on divorce.
  • Completeness of Protocol: Agreement must be reached on all collateral consequences such as alimony, compensation, and custody.

Who can file for divorce?

An uncontested divorce case can be filed by the spouses applying together to the court. In addition, it can take the "uncontested" state by the other spouse accepting a contested divorce case filed by one spouse or by the parties reaching an agreement during the process.

Against whom is an uncontested divorce filed?

Since the case is in the nature of an "application" rather than a technical dispute, the spouses rely on each other's consent. It is a special divorce procedure containing non-contentious elements.

Which is the competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Family Court. The authorized court is the court of the place of residence of one of the spouses. However, since an objection to jurisdiction is generally not made in an uncontested divorce, it can also be filed in the court of the place the parties agreed upon.

Is there a time limit for uncontested divorce?

The most critical period is that at least 1 year must have passed since the marriage. In marriages that have not completed 1 year, the route of uncontested divorce cannot be taken; in this case, only a contested divorce case can be filed.

What should be done before filing for an uncontested divorce?

Completing these preparations before filing the case ensures that the case ends in a single session:

  • Preparation of a legally valid Divorce Protocol.
  • Submission of the lawsuit petition and the protocol to the court completely and without deficiency.
  • Clear expression of alimony, custody, and property division items in the protocol.

How does the uncontested divorce process progress?

  1. A lawsuit is filed with the Family Court with the prepared petition and protocol.
  2. A hearing date is given by the court (In some regions, a date is given in a very short time).
  3. Both spouses personally attend the hearing at the determined day and time.
  4. The judge examines that the wills of the parties are free and the situation of the children in the protocol.
  5. When the necessary conditions are met, the judge decides on divorce in the same session.

How long does an uncontested divorce take?

In practice, uncontested divorce cases generally result in 1 week to 1 month, usually on the day of the hearing (in a single session). Including the writing and finalization of the decision, the process is quite fast.
