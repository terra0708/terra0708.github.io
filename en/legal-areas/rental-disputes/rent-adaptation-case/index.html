Rent Adaptation Lawsuit: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Rental Disputes Rent Adaptation

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the rent adaptation (kira uyarlama) lawsuit filed to redetermine the rent or agreement conditions in case the balance between the parties is disturbed due to extraordinary situations arising after the conclusion of the lease agreement. This lawsuit aims to restore the agreement balance within the scope of "excessive difficulty of performance" provisions regulated in Article 138 of the Turkish Code of Obligations.

1. What is a rent adaptation lawsuit?

A rent adaptation lawsuit is a case filed for the court to redetermine the rent or agreement conditions if the economic balance between the parties is disturbed due to extraordinary situations arising after the lease agreement is established. With this lawsuit:

  • The rent is adapted according to current conditions.
  • The balance between the parties is restored.
  • The sustainability of the agreement is protected.

This matter is based on Article 138 of the Turkish Code of Obligations (Excessive Difficulty of Performance).

2. In which cases is it filed?

A rent adaptation case is particularly considered in the following situations:

  • Extraordinary situations such as economic crisis or hyperinflation.
  • Unpredictable and extreme changes in exchange rates.
  • Agreement balance being disturbed to an unbearable degree against one party.
  • Rent amount becoming excessively heavy for one of the parties.

However, for this lawsuit to be filed, the situation must be unpredictable, extraordinary, and not caused by the debtor.

3. Who can file this lawsuit?

Both parties have the right to file this lawsuit:

  • The lessor (if the agreement conditions have become very low).
  • The tenant (if the agreement conditions have become unbearably high).

4. Against whom is it filed?

The lawsuit is filed against the other party of the agreement:

  • By the landlord against the tenant.
  • By the tenant against the landlord.

5. Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Civil Court of Peace (Sulh Hukuk Mahkemesi). The authorized court is the court where the immovable property is located.

6. Is there a time limit?

There is no specific statutory time limit in the law for rent adaptation cases. However:

  • The request for adaptation can be made as long as the extraordinary situation continues.
  • The timing of the lawsuit can determine the impact of the requested adaptation.

7. What to do before filing?

  • Detailed analysis of the current lease agreement.
  • Documentation of extraordinary economic changes.
  • Researching comparable rent values in the area.
  • Final evaluation of a settlement (agreement) possibility between the parties.

8. How does the process progress?

  1. A lawsuit petition is submitted to the Civil Court of Peace.
  2. Statements and evidence of the parties are collected.
  3. General economic conditions and regional precedents are examined.
  4. Court-appointed experts (sector specialists) conduct an examination.
  5. The judge rules on the new agreement amount based on equity.

9. How long does it take?

Depending on the evidence and expert phases, it generally takes 6 months to 1 year in practice.
