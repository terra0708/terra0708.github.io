Rent Determination Lawsuit: What is it and How to File?

Home Legal Areas Rental Disputes Rent Determination

In this article, we aim to provide basic information about the rent determination (kira tespit) lawsuit filed to adjust the rent determined in the lease agreement to the economic conditions of the day. This lawsuit is regulated under Articles 344 and 345 of the Turkish Code of Obligations and ensures that the rent is determined in accordance with equity. In this context, issues most wondered in practice such as what a rent determination case is, in what cases it can be filed, by whom it can be filed, which court is competent, and how the litigation process progresses are addressed.

What is a rent determination lawsuit?

A rent determination lawsuit is a declaratory action filed to redetermine the rent set in the lease agreement in accordance with current economic conditions and comparable rental values.

With this lawsuit:

  • The rent is redetermined by the court.
  • Rental balance between the parties is ensured.
  • It is aimed to reach the fair market rental value.

This matter is based on Turkish Code of Obligations Art. 344 and 345.

In which cases is it filed?

It is particularly filed in the following situations:

  • The rent remaining low or high according to market conditions
  • Failure to reach an agreement on rent increase between the parties
  • The amount losing its currency in long-term lease agreements

Who can file this lawsuit?

A rent determination case can be filed by both the lessor (landlord) and the tenant.

Against whom is it filed?

It is filed by the landlord against the tenant, or by the tenant against the landlord. It is a contested litigation matter.

Competent and authorized court?

The competent court is the Civil Court of Peace (Sulh Hukuk Mahkemesi). The authorized court is the court of the place where the leased property is located.

Is there a time limit for a rent determination case?

The important issue is which rental period the lawsuit will apply to:

  • If the lawsuit is filed at least 30 days before the new rental period or a notice has been served within this period, the determined rent will apply in the new period.
  • Otherwise, the decision becomes valid in the following rental period.
  • In lease agreements that have completed 5 years, the rent can be redetermined according to market precedents (TCO Art. 344).

What to do before filing?

  • Detailed examination of the lease agreement clauses
  • Researching comparable rental values in the area
  • Sending a notice before the new period if necessary

How does the process progress?

  1. A lawsuit is filed in the Civil Court of Peace.
  2. Statements and evidence of the parties are collected.
  3. Comparable rent research is conducted in the region.
  4. Expert (real estate specialist) examination is carried out.
  5. The court determines the rent amount.

The court makes a decision by taking into account equity reduction and comparable rental values.

How long does it take?

The duration varies, but results are generally achieved within 6 months to 1 year. Expert examinations can extend this timeframe.
